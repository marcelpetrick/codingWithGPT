**→ Pick `2:1` France.** France is the clear favourite in a game that is

high-scoring favourite win, gets the exact on 2–1 and GD credit on 1–0/3–2, and
aligns with the Mbappé-scores scenario that keeps the top-scorer bonus flickering.

**→ Pick `2:1` Spain.** Spain is a genuine, market-clear favourite. Argentina

(and it banks GD credit on a 1–0). This is the higher-EV chalk line and it keeps
us moving *with* the field rather than diverging.

**The variance lever (decide AFTER the bonus scout).** Note Spain to win is only
**~40% at 90 min** — the single most likely outcome, but a minority one (draw 30%,
ARG 28%). So chalk `2:1 Spain` protects our tie-break-backed 4th but rarely
*gains* on a field that also picks Spain. Two scenarios:

- **If P03/P04 hold a dead (France) champion bonus like us** → chalk is enough:
  match the field, let one exact + our 1.83 tie-break climb us to 2nd/3rd. **Play
  `2:1 Spain`.**
- **If P03/P04 hold a live (Spain/Argentina) champion bonus** → they add +4 in
  the final and chalk *cannot* catch them. The only route to the podium is a
  **differentiated, correct final call** — back **Argentina to win** (`1:2`;
  ~44% over the full match as the holders/Messi/comeback side, and it separates
  us from the Spain-picking field). Cost: if Spain win we score 0 and risk
  dropping to 5th (P06 tied) — acceptable only because holding 4th off the podium
  has little prize value.

**Default recommendation: `2:1 Spain`** (highest single-outcome probability,
protects position). Switch to `1:2 Argentina` **only** if the bonus scout shows
the rivals directly ahead can't be caught any other way.
