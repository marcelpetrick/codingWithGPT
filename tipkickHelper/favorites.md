## 🇫🇷 France — co-favorite, peak Deschamps farewell

**Squad announced 14 May 2026** (26 players).
**The case for**: Reigning final-runners-up; still the deepest attack
in the field — **Mbappé + Dembélé (reigning Ballon d'Or) + Doué +
Olise + Cherki + Barcola + Thuram + Mateta**. Proven tournament
knowhow. Deschamps publicly declared this his last tournament —
historically Deschamps-led teams over-perform in swansong years.
Beat Brazil 2–1 (March, with 10 men!), 3–1 Colombia in pre-WC friendlies.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-08)**:
- Surprise squad cuts: **Camavinga left out** (poor season form), **Kolo
  Muani left out** / **Ekitike OUT** (Achilles rupture, replaced by Mateta).
- **William Saliba (CB)** — back injury post-UCL final (Arsenal lost to PSG).
  **✅ CLEARED June 8**: Deschamps confirmed "if he had to play tomorrow he
  would play." Saliba on a managed programme; expected to play vs Northern
  Ireland (June 8) for match sharpness. Targeting Senegal opener June 16.
- **Kylian Mbappé** — semitendinosus muscle injury (left leg, late April).
  **Managed carefully**: played 45 min vs Ivory Coast June 4 (left at HT as
  precaution). Expected to start vs Northern Ireland June 8 and lead the line.
  Still being monitored but no longer a concern for the opener.
- **Friendly form concern**: France lost **1–2 to Ivory Coast** (June 4) in
  Nantes — Cherki opened, Doué + Amad scored in second half with a rotated
  side. First warm-up loss since pre-tournament form matters to Polymarket;
  market dipped 1 pt. Context: heavily rotated for the second half.
- Group I is the "Group of Death": Senegal (AFCON champs, Mané/Jackson/Sarr)
  and Norway (Haaland 16 qualifying goals, 8/8 unbeaten) are not makeweights.
**Group**: I (Senegal, Norway, Iraq).
**Verdict**: ★★★★★ — Saliba cleared and Mbappé tracking fine; the June 4
loss was with a half-strength side. Model favorite unchanged.

## 🇪🇸 Spain — Euro 2024 champs, "system" team

**The case for**: Have not lost a competitive match since March 2024.
Just two losses since start of 2023. Tactical clarity, deep central
midfield (Pedri, Fabián, Zubimendi, Gavi), Yamal/Williams width.
ESPN's PR #1.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-08)**:
- **Lamine Yamal** — partial hamstring tear (April 22). **Status improving**:
  De la Fuente confirmed "he's doing good" and expects him ready for the June
  15 opener vs Cabo Verde. Deliberately left out of Spain's Peru warmup
  (June 8) — Yamal + Nico Williams + Víctor Muñoz all staying at base camp
  in Chattanooga to continue rehabilitation. Coach: "If nothing changes he
  could be ready June 15, though not guaranteed he'll play." Day-to-day;
  targeting game 2 vs Saudi Arabia (June 20) as the safe milestone.
- **Fermín López (MF)** — fractured 5th metatarsal, surgery May 25. **Full
  tournament out**; not a starter loss but reduces midfield depth.
- **Mikel Merino (MF)** — stress fracture foot since February. Still
  doubtful; hasn't played since Feb. Unlikely to start opener.
- **No Real Madrid players** selected at all — notable given Madrid's UCL run.
**Group**: H (Uruguay, Saudi Arabia, Cabo Verde) — Uruguay is a real
test for top of group.
**Verdict**: ★★★★★ — slight model #2 only because of Yamal risk.

## 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 England — Tuchel reset

**The case for**: Talent surplus (Bellingham, Saka, Foden, Palmer,
Rice, Kane). Tuchel won a Champions League and is a true tournament
tactician. England got the second-easiest run on paper.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-08)**:
- **Lost 0–1 to Japan at Wembley (March 31)** — first-ever England loss to
  Asian opposition. Tuchel's attack still looks blunt in warm-ups.
- **Notable exclusions**: Grealish OUT (injury), Ben White OUT (injury), Phil
  Foden excluded (form), Cole Palmer excluded (Tuchel's selection).
- **Tino Livramento (RB)** — thigh injury, ended club season April 18. In squad
  but unlikely to start opener vs Croatia (June 17). Match fitness very low.
- **Friendly results**: **England 1–0 New Zealand (June 6)** — Kane scored in
  first-half stoppage time. Tuchel used 22 players, rotated heavily at HT.
  A professional win but not convincing as a performance. Final warmup vs
  Costa Rica in Orlando (June 10).
- Group L includes **Croatia**, who have specialized in deflating England's
  hopes for a decade. Modrić still on the pitch.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — talent is there; the Tuchel/system question is unresolved.

**The case against — significant (updated 2026-06-08)**:

**Group**: C with Morocco — the 2022 QF rematch in groups. 1–1 pick holds;
Neymar absence for game 1 is now very likely confirmed.
**Verdict**: ★★★ — still chalk for semi-final under Strategy A; drifted to +900.

## 🇦🇷 Argentina — defending champion, Messi farewell

**The case for**: Most cohesive squad in the world. **Three trophies
in a row** (Copa 2021, WC 2022, Copa 2024) under Scaloni. Messi's
playing time at Inter Miami managed carefully — he'll be fresh.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-08) — injury cluster easing:**
- **Lionel Messi** — hamstring fatigue/mild strain (left leg). **Positive
  update June 6–7**: Messi has **rejoined partial group training** — coach
  Scaloni confirmed "Leo is doing well, he's already trained with the group
  for part of the session and is no longer training separately." Sat out
  Honduras friendly (June 6); Argentina won 2–0 via Lautaro Martínez pen +
  Giuliano Simeone. May get a few minutes vs Iceland (June 9). Expected fit
  June 16 opener vs Algeria.
- **Emiliano Martínez (GK)** — fractured finger. Sitting out friendlies as
  precaution. Medical staff confident for WC.
- **Cristian Romero (CB)** — lateral ligament sprain. No surgery; expected fit.
- **Nahuel Molina (RB)** — thigh injury, May. Race to be fit.
- **Gonzalo Montiel (RB)** — muscle injury, recovering.
Overall injury picture improved significantly since June 2. Messi rejoining
group sessions is the key marker. Squad is ageing (Messi 38, Otamendi 38).
**Friendly results**: Argentina **2–0 Honduras** (June 6) at Kyle Field, Texas.
Lautaro (pen, 37') + Giuliano Simeone (early 2H). Professional display.
Final warmup vs Iceland — June 9, Jordan-Hare Stadium, Auburn.
**Group**: J (Algeria, Austria, Jordan) — Austria is the real test.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — injury cloud largely lifted; still chalk. Monitor Messi
vs Iceland for the final fitness signal.

## 🇵🇹 Portugal — sleeping giant

**The case for**: Possibly the deepest squad in the field after France.
Bernardo, Bruno Fernandes, Vitinha, Rúben Neves, Leão, Félix, Diogo
Jota, plus Nuno Mendes/Cancelo/Dias. Roberto Martínez has a year+ of
continuity now. RotoWire's model pick.
**The case against**: Group K with **Colombia** is genuinely tough.
Portugal has historically under-performed at World Cups vs. Euros.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — +1000 is the value bet of the top 8.

## Headline takeaways

- **Top 5 (France, Spain, England, Brazil, Argentina) are correctly
  priced** — implied probabilities sum to about 70%.
- The **value bet** is Portugal at +1000.
- **Spain is the most reliable team**, but **France has the highest
  ceiling** given Deschamps' "last dance" effect.
- **Don't pick Germany or Netherlands as your champion** — both have
  meaningful path-of-bracket / form risks.
