## 🇫🇷 France — co-favorite, peak Deschamps farewell

**Squad announced 14 May 2026** (26 players).
**The case for**: Reigning final-runners-up; still the deepest attack
in the field — **Mbappé + Dembélé (reigning Ballon d'Or) + Doué +
Olise + Cherki + Barcola + Thuram + Mateta**. Proven tournament
knowhow. Deschamps publicly declared this his last tournament —
historically Deschamps-led teams over-perform in swansong years.
Beat Brazil 2–1 (March, with 10 men!), 3–1 Colombia in pre-WC friendlies.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-02)**:
- Surprise squad cuts: **Camavinga left out** (poor season form), **Kolo
  Muani left out** / **Ekitike OUT** (Achilles rupture, replaced by Mateta).
- **William Saliba (CB)** — back injury post-UCL final (Arsenal lost to PSG).
  Scans "reassuring" but FFF internally worried. Opener vs Senegal is June
  16; time is tight. Doubtful for game 1.
- **Kylian Mbappé** — semitendinosus muscle injury (left leg, late April).
  Discomfort in training as of June 1. Still expected to feature but France
  actively monitoring. **Not fully cleared.**
- Group I is the "Group of Death": Senegal (AFCON champs, Mané/Jackson/Sarr)
  and Norway (Haaland 16 qualifying goals, 8/8 unbeaten) are not makeweights.
**Group**: I (Senegal, Norway, Iraq).
**Verdict**: ★★★★★ — still the model favorite; injuries are a watch, not a demotion.

## 🇪🇸 Spain — Euro 2024 champs, "system" team

**The case for**: Have not lost a competitive match since March 2024.
Just two losses since start of 2023. Tactical clarity, deep central
midfield (Pedri, Fabián, Zubimendi, Gavi), Yamal/Williams width.
ESPN's PR #1.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-02)**:
- **Lamine Yamal** — partial hamstring tear (April 22). De la Fuente
  "would risk saying he'll be ready for game 1" (vs Cabo Verde, June 15),
  but Yamal himself admitted he feared missing the WC entirely. Status still
  day-to-day; Spain vs Iraq friendly (June 4, result: 3–0) played without him.
- **Fermín López (MF)** — fractured 5th metatarsal, surgery May 25. **Full
  tournament out**; not a starter loss but reduces midfield depth.
- **Mikel Merino (MF)** — stress fracture in foot since February; targeted
  UCL final to prove fitness. Status unclear.
- **No Real Madrid players** selected at all — notable given Madrid's UCL run.
**Group**: H (Uruguay, Saudi Arabia, Cabo Verde) — Uruguay is a real
test for top of group.
**Verdict**: ★★★★★ — slight model #2 only because of Yamal risk.

## 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 England — Tuchel reset

**The case for**: Talent surplus (Bellingham, Saka, Foden, Palmer,
Rice, Kane). Tuchel won a Champions League and is a true tournament
tactician. England got the second-easiest run on paper.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-02)**:
- **Lost 0–1 to Japan at Wembley (March 31)** — first-ever England loss to
  Asian opposition. Tuchel's attack looks blunt; Palmer and Bellingham not
  yet clicking as a unit.
- **Notable exclusions**: Grealish OUT (injury), Ben White OUT (injury), Phil
  Foden excluded (form), Cole Palmer excluded (Tuchel's selection).
- **Tino Livramento (RB)** — thigh injury, ended club season April 18. In squad
  but unlikely to start opener vs Croatia (June 17). Match fitness very low.
- Squad departed to Florida prep camp June 1. England vs New Zealand (June 6)
  and Costa Rica (June 10) are the last fitness tests.
- Group L includes **Croatia**, who have specialized in deflating England's
  hopes for a decade. Modrić still on the pitch.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — talent is there; the Tuchel/system question is unresolved.

## 🇧🇷 Brazil — Ancelotti reset (squad weakened)

**Squad announced 18 May 2026**.
**The case for**: First non-Brazilian head coach in 60+ years —
Ancelotti brings tournament pedigree. Vinícius, Raphinha, Endrick,
Bruno Guimarães — and **Neymar IN** after 2.5 years out.
**The case against — significant (updated 2026-06-02)**:
- **Rodrygo OUT** (ACL + meniscus surgery in March; back ~Nov 2026).
- **Éder Militão OUT** (April hamstring surgery; 5+ months out).
- **Estêvão OUT** (torn hamstring; Bremer/Fabrício Bruno step up as CBs).
- **Neymar — grade 2 calf strain (May 17)**. Ancelotti included him anyway.
  Expected return 2–3 weeks from injury date → **likely misses June 13 opener
  vs Morocco**, potentially available for later group games. "No regrets" taking
  him, but effectively a game-2-or-3 player at best.
- Defense in transition: Marquinhos ageing, Militão gone, no elite CB pairing.
**Form**: **6–2 vs Panama** at Maracaña (June 1) — Vinícius scored in 2', dominant
display. Morale boost despite the missing stars.
**Group**: C with Morocco — the 2022 QF rematch in groups. Your 1–1 pick holds.
**Verdict**: ★★★ — still chalk for semi-final under Strategy A; +850 priced fairly.

## 🇦🇷 Argentina — defending champion, Messi farewell

**The case for**: Most cohesive squad in the world. **Three trophies
in a row** (Copa 2021, WC 2022, Copa 2024) under Scaloni. Messi's
playing time at Inter Miami managed carefully — he'll be fresh.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-02) — significant injury cluster**:
- **Lionel Messi** — hamstring overload/muscle fatigue, on individual
  training programme in Kansas. Skipping friendlies vs Honduras/Iceland.
  Insider Edul says WILL be fit for June 16 opener vs Algeria. Monitor daily.
- **Emiliano Martínez (GK)** — fractured right-hand finger (Europa League
  final). Sitting out friendlies as precaution. Medical staff confident for WC.
- **Cristian Romero (CB)** — lateral ligament sprain, right knee (mid-April).
  No surgery; expected fit.
- **Nahuel Molina (RB)** — thigh injury, May. Race to be fit.
- **Gonzalo Montiel (RB)** — muscle injury, recovering.
Argentina entered June with all five of the above in the treatment room
simultaneously. All are projected fit for June 16, but this is a fragile state.
Squad is ageing (Messi 38, Otamendi 38, Di María retired). ESPN ranked
Argentina 2nd most likely favorite to crash out early.
**Group**: J (Algeria, Austria, Jordan) — Austria is the real test.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — still chalk; monitor Messi daily until June 16.

## 🇵🇹 Portugal — sleeping giant

**The case for**: Possibly the deepest squad in the field after France.
Bernardo, Bruno Fernandes, Vitinha, Rúben Neves, Leão, Félix, Diogo
Jota, plus Nuno Mendes/Cancelo/Dias. Roberto Martínez has a year+ of
continuity now. RotoWire's model pick.
**The case against**: Group K with **Colombia** is genuinely tough.
Portugal has historically under-performed at World Cups vs. Euros.
**Verdict**: ★★★★ — +1000 is the value bet of the top 8.

**Squad announced 21 May 2026** (planned).
**The case for**: Nagelsmann's project has matured since Euro 2024
(QF, lost narrowly to Spain). Wirtz (FFT's #1 attacking midfielder
in the world right now), Sané, Havertz, Kimmich.
**The case against (updated 2026-06-02)**:
- **Gnabry OUT** (torn adductor, mid-April; confirmed via Instagram).
- **Ter Stegen CONFIRMED OUT** (torn thigh muscle; not in final squad).
  **Manuel Neuer returned from international retirement as confirmed No. 1.**
- Notable non-injury exclusions: Füllkrug (1 Serie A goal at Milan), Adeyemi

- Defense still the weakest of the top 8.
**Form**: **4–0 vs Finland (May 31)** — Undav brace, Wirtz/Musiala firing;

**Verdict**: ★★★ — form is encouraging; +1400 is still too short at this price.

## 🇳🇱 Netherlands

**The case for**: Talented but inconsistent. Koeman knows tournaments.
**The case against**: Group F with **Japan** and Sweden is the hardest
group of any top-10 side. Could finish 2nd or even 3rd.
**Verdict**: ★★ — pass at +2000.

## Headline takeaways

- **Top 5 (France, Spain, England, Brazil, Argentina) are correctly
  priced** — implied probabilities sum to about 70%.
- The **value bet** is Portugal at +1000.
- **Spain is the most reliable team**, but **France has the highest
  ceiling** given Deschamps' "last dance" effect.
- **Don't pick Germany or Netherlands as your champion** — both have
  meaningful path-of-bracket / form risks.
