## Why we finished 5th, not on the podium

Honest post-mortem — two causes, in order of impact:

1. **Two forgotten R16 entries.** A missing entry is a guaranteed 0. At our
   median rate (~2 pts/match) those two games were worth **~3–4 points**. Add
   them back and we land on **~231–232 — level with 4th (232) and one point off
   the podium (233).** This single lapse, not any prediction, is the difference
   between 5th and a podium. Full analysis in [`missedGames.md`](missedGames.md).
2. **An unpredictable endgame.** The knockout finish broke the chalk model:
   **both semifinal favourites (France and England) lost**, and the final-matchday
   picks scored 0. No sound strategy modelled a France/England double collapse —
   this was variance, not a method failure.

## Verdict — a win

- **The strategy was sound.** Chalk-led picking built a 168-point match haul and
  a 3rd-place position deep into the tournament; the model called the group and
  early-knockout stages well.
- **The goal was to get the money back, not to finish 1st.** 5th place returns
  the €10 entry as **€28 — a profit.** Objective met.
- **The one fixable lesson for next time:** never skip an entry. Two saved tips
  would have turned a profitable 5th into a podium finish.

Not sour — it worked out. Cash returned, strategy validated, and the only real
mistake (forgotten entries) is fully in our control next time.
