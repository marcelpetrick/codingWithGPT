# Host Nations & Home Advantage

> "Six of the 22 World Cup tournaments have been won by the host nation"
> — but no host has won since France 1998. South Africa 2010 and Qatar
> 2022 were both **group-stage exits**.

## What history actually says

- **Host nations win ~27 %** of the time (6/22): Uruguay '30, Italy '34,
  England '66, West Germany '74, Argentina '78, France '98.
- **13 of 22** hosts have at least reached the semi-finals.
- The home-advantage effect has **shrunk** since 1998 — travel and
  preparation gaps used to be huge; today, every elite team plays in
  globalized leagues.
- The "boost" is real but not as large as nostalgia suggests. Modern
  hosts typically **outperform their ranking by ~half a round**.

## Caveat for 2026: three co-hosts

- This is the **first three-nation host**. Home advantage is diluted
  three ways and varies by city.
- 60 of 104 matches are in the USA, so **USA gets the strongest home
  effect**; Mexico in the early Mexico City / Guadalajara matches
  (altitude!); Canada the smallest boost (only 13 matches, 2 cities).

## 🇺🇸 USA — Mauricio Pochettino's Team

**Group D**: USA, Paraguay, Australia, Türkiye.

**Form (March 2026 break)**: Lost to Belgium 4–1 and Portugal 3–1
(aggregate 7–2). Fans and pundits criticized rotation and tactical
indecision. Former USMNT keeper Kasey Keller publicly said
Pochettino "doesn't know his best lineup."

**Pochettino approach**: Has invited 45+ players to camp since
September 2024; routinely benches Pulisic and Adams. His tactic is
"no one is safe" — strong on paper, but with only ~4 windows before
the tournament it's risky.

**Injuries**: **Johnny Cardoso (DM)** is expected to **miss the World
Cup** with an ankle injury — a real blow given how thin USMNT is at
the 6.

**Verdict**: The talent is real (Pulisic, Reyna, McKennie, Adams,
Balogun, Weah). The cohesion is not. **Floor: group exit. Ceiling:
quarter-final.** Most likely: R16, knocked out by an in-form European
side. Don't pick USA past QF.

## 🇲🇽 Mexico — Javier Aguirre's Team

**Group A**: Mexico, South Africa, Korea Republic, Czechia (open the
tournament in Mexico City at altitude).

**Recent**: Won both the **2025 Concacaf Nations League** and
**Gold Cup** under Aguirre's third stint. **Chucky Lozano left out**
of the preliminary squad — sign of a generational reset.

**Edge**: All group games at altitude (Mexico City 2,240 m, Guadalajara
1,560 m) — Mexico are physically adapted; opponents are not. Opening
the tournament in Azteca is the single biggest single-game advantage
any team has.

**Ceiling**: Quarter-final at best. Mexico has lost in R16 in **seven
straight** World Cups (1994–2018) — the "**Quinto Partido**" curse.

**Verdict**: Lock for top-2 in Group A. R16 likely; QF is the realistic
ceiling. Bonus-pick value: useful as "Group A winner" pick.

## 🇨🇦 Canada — Jesse Marsch's Team

**Group B**: Canada, Switzerland, Qatar, Bosnia.

**Edge**: Easiest group of any host. Marsch is American-style intense
press, which suits this squad. Davies fully fit again (ACL 2024).
Group games in Toronto + Vancouver — friendly weather, big crowds.

**Talent**: Alphonso Davies (LB → wing), Tajon Buchanan, Jonathan David
(top-3 striker), Stephen Eustáquio. Real golden generation.

**Verdict (updated 2026-06-09)**: Top-2 in Group B is realistic.
**R16 likely; QF is a stretch but achievable.** Canada remains a live
host-advantage team, but the June 9 group-winner market now favors
Switzerland clearly (55 % vs Canada 33 %), so Canada is no longer the
Strategy A group-winner default.

## How to actually price the home advantage in your picks

| Round target | USA | Mexico | Canada |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Group stage (advance) | ~85 % | ~90 % | ~70 % |
| Round of 16 | ~55 % | ~60 % | ~45 % |
| Quarter-final | ~20 % | ~15 % | ~12 % |
| Semi-final | ~5 % | ~3 % | ~3 % |
| Final | ~2 % | <1 % | <1 % |

(These are my own subjective estimates; market is similar.)

**My take**: No host wins this. But there's a non-trivial chance
**one host reaches the semi-final**. Canada is still playable for
"advance from group" / R16, but not as the safe Group B winner pick.

If you want a contrarian bonus pick: **Canada to reach the
quarter-final** is worth thinking about.
