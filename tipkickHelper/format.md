# Format & Schedule

## The new 48-team format

For the first time, **48 teams** play (up from 32 in 2022). They are
divided into **12 groups of 4** (A–L).

- **Group stage**: 3 matches each → 36 group matches.
- **Round of 32** (new!): top 2 from each group + **8 best 3rd-placed**
  teams advance. 32 teams, 16 matches.
- **Round of 16, Quarter-Final, Semi-Final, 3rd-place, Final**: standard
  single-elimination.
- **Total matches**: 104 (up from 64 in 2022).

This means the **eight worst third-placed teams go home**, but most
"big names" who slip up in their group still get a second chance — a
bigger cushion for favorites than in previous formats.

## Key consequence for picks

- More group games = more chance for upsets in the **group stage**,
  but the **Round of 32 safety net** softens the impact.
- Six teams play group games in altitude (Mexico City ≈ 2,240 m,
  Guadalajara ≈ 1,560 m). This matters: cardio-heavy pressing teams
  (Spain, Germany, USMNT) may suffer at Azteca; Mexico are adapted.
- Travel is brutal. Teams placed across multiple US time zones early
  could be at a disadvantage vs. teams with a localized group draw.
