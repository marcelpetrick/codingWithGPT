# DATA MODUL AG

**Hiring fit:** No - strong embedded/display business, but recent employer-review signals are too weak for a confident hiring recommendation.

## Company snapshot

DATA MODUL AG is a Munich-based public company founded in 1972. It develops, integrates, and distributes professional display, touch, embedded, panel PC, industrial monitor, controller board, carrier board, ARM/x86 computing, and system-integration solutions. It operates internationally with more than 20 locations and production/logistics/administration capacity.

For embedded applicants, the technical fit is real: displays, industrial HMIs, embedded computing, carrier boards, touch technology, optical bonding, CPU boards, industrial computers, and custom display systems.

## Size, revenue, and growth

DATA MODUL reported about **EUR 212.88M** revenue in 2025, down **5.89%** year over year according to market data. StockAnalysis lists **508 employees**. LinkedIn lists the company in the **501-1,000 employees** band.

## Work culture and applicant signal

The technical business is stronger than the employer signal. Kununu shows about **3.5/5**, but only **36% recommendation** over the last two years. Recent comments cite weak communication, management issues, limited development feedback, work-life balance concerns, and perceived lack of transparency. Glassdoor is a smaller sample, but also weak at about **2.9/5** and low recommendation.

There are positives: colleague cohesion, flexible hours in some teams, and interesting display/embedded products. Still, for a hiring recommendation, the current review trend is concerning. Applicants should be very careful, validate the exact team, ask about recent turnover, salary progression, working hours, management style, and whether the company is still affected by cost-saving measures or short-time work.

## Market outlook

The market is stable but competitive. Industrial displays, HMIs, medical displays, EV charging interfaces, MiniLED, embedded systems, and custom industrial visualization remain relevant. However, 2025 revenue decline suggests pressure in the current cycle.
