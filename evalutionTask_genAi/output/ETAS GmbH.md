# ETAS GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Yes - major automotive software employer with strong market relevance and generally good reviews, though senior-management signals are mixed.

## Company snapshot

ETAS GmbH is a Stuttgart-based Bosch subsidiary founded in 1994. It develops vehicle software, middleware, development tools, cloud services, cybersecurity solutions, AUTOSAR tooling, measurement/calibration, ECU development tools, diagnostics, virtual testing, and software-defined vehicle platforms.

For embedded and automotive software applicants, ETAS is one of the strongest domain fits in the list: deeply embedded software, AUTOSAR, vehicle middleware, calibration, test/validation, DevOps for automotive, cybersecurity, diagnostics, and SDV infrastructure.

## Size, revenue, and growth

ETAS reports approximately **2,700 associates**, **39 locations in 14 countries**, and **EUR 542M sales in 2025**. LinkedIn lists the company in the **1,001-5,000 employees** range.

## Work culture and applicant signal

Kununu shows about **4.3/5** with **74% recommendation** over the last two years; employee-only comments show about **4.1/5** and **82% recommendation**. Glassdoor is solid at about **3.8-3.9/5** with **70% recommendation**. Positive signals include pay/benefits, work-life balance, remote availability, interesting technology, Bosch backing, and broad career options. Negative signals include silos, slow decisions, leadership churn, organizational complexity, and mixed senior-management approval.

This is a strong target for software engineering managers, especially in automotive software, embedded platforms, AUTOSAR, middleware, cybersecurity, or SDV tooling. For Munich specifically, verify whether the role is remote/hybrid from Stuttgart, in a Bosch/ETAS Munich site, or requires relocation/commuting.

## Market outlook

The outlook is strong. Software-defined vehicles, AUTOSAR, cybersecurity, vehicle health, DevOps, virtual validation, and automotive cloud services are strategic industry priorities.
