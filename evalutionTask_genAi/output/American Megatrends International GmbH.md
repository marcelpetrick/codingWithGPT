# American Megatrends International GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - strong firmware market and global brand, but compensation and career signals are mixed.

## Applicant Summary

American Megatrends International GmbH is the Munich presence of AMI, the global firmware company formerly known as American Megatrends. AMI focuses on UEFI/BIOS firmware, BMC/server manageability firmware, platform security, embedded/IoT, data center, edge, and AI infrastructure platforms. For firmware, BIOS, BMC, embedded Linux, platform security, or server-management roles, the technical market fit is strong. The caution is employer feedback: Glassdoor reviews are generally acceptable, but recurring negatives include low compensation, politics, and uneven management.

## Location And Departments

- German location: Truderinger Strasse 283, 81825 Munich, Germany.
- Global headquarters: Duluth/Gwinnett County, Georgia, USA.
- Departments/work areas: UEFI/BIOS firmware, Aptio, MegaRAC BMC/manageability, platform security/Tektagon, embedded/IoT firmware, data-center and AI infrastructure firmware, customer engineering, sales, consulting, and support.
- Remote/mobile work: Glassdoor reviews mention flexible hours and some remote support, but policy appears team/region dependent.

## Revenue And Growth

AMI is private and does not publish reliable public revenue. Public growth indicators are strategic rather than financial.

- Founded in 1985.
- Wikipedia and public profiles report **1,000+** worldwide employees.
- AMI received a major investment from HGGC in 2019 and rebranded around Power / Manage / Secure.
- AMI's current positioning targets AI data centers, cloud, edge, embedded/IoT, open-source firmware, and platform security.

Interpretation: financially opaque but strategically relevant. The company sits in a critical supply chain for computing infrastructure, firmware security, and server manageability.

## Market And Goals

The market is strong. Firmware, BMC management, secure boot, root-of-trust, AI server platforms, and open-source firmware are becoming more important as data centers and edge systems scale. AMI claims its firmware powers billions of devices and positions itself as a low-risk partner for critical computing platforms.

## Employee Satisfaction

- Glassdoor AMI: about 3.6-3.7 rating from 364-365 reviews, 71% recommendation, 84% CEO approval, and 65% positive business outlook.
- Positive themes: work-life balance, friendly teams, flexible hours, strong entry-level learning in BIOS/firmware, deep system-level exposure.
- Negative themes: low salary, below-average raises, corporate politics, uneven management, slower growth after the first learning phase.
- Pay and salary increases: recurring negative signal; compensation/benefits category is lower than culture/work-life.
- Ambitious people: good for deep firmware/system software learning, especially early career; less convincing for people who want fast compensation growth or modern big-tech-style career paths.
- Family friendliness: likely moderate because work-life balance is generally rated decently, but this should be validated for the Munich team.

## Decision

Good company for hiring? **Maybe.** It is attractive for firmware and platform-security engineers who want deep systems work and a globally relevant product area. I would negotiate compensation carefully and ask direct questions about raises, manager quality, technical roadmap, remote policy, and whether the Munich role is engineering-heavy or mostly customer-facing.
