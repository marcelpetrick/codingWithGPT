# d.stream engineering

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - highly relevant DSP, embedded vision, and SoC work, but very small team and little independent employer-review data.

## Company snapshot

d.stream engineering is a Berlin-based embedded engineering service provider focused on digital signal processing, embedded systems, Vision AI, camera systems, ADAS, radar, robotics, audio/video processing, IoT, and SoC/DSP platforms. The company was founded around 2003 and presents more than 20-25 years of project experience.

For embedded applicants, the technical fit is strong: C/C++, DSPs, SoCs, RTOS, embedded Linux, drivers, bootloaders, algorithm optimization, real-time performance, functional safety, and series-production support.

## Size, revenue, and growth

LinkedIn lists d.stream in the **11-50 employees** range, while the company text describes a team of about **10** developers. Public revenue is not disclosed. This is a small specialist engineering company, so growth should be evaluated through project pipeline and customer stability rather than public financials.

## Work culture and applicant signal

There is not enough independent employer-review data to make a strong culture call. The company describes a coordinated team of experienced developers, transparent cooperation, long-running project knowledge, and hands-on lab capability. The small-team setup may offer direct technical ownership and learning from senior embedded specialists.

Risks are typical for small service providers: dependency on customer projects, limited internal career ladder, potentially variable workload, and fewer benefits than larger product companies. Applicants should ask about contract pipeline, remote policy, overtime, senior mentoring, salary, and whether the role is internal product work or customer delivery.

## Market outlook

The market outlook is good for the technical niche. Embedded vision, edge AI, camera systems, robotics, ADAS, railway/IoT monitoring, and optimized real-time software remain relevant. The business model is more project/service-driven than scalable product software.
