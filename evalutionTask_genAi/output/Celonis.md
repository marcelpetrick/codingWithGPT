# Celonis

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - strong German software scale-up and market position, but workload and management-pressure signals need careful checking.

## Company snapshot

Celonis is an enterprise software company founded in 2011 and headquartered in Munich and New York. It builds a Process Intelligence Platform based on process mining, process analytics, automation, AI, and business process optimization. It serves large enterprises and says it has helped **1,000+** major companies, with more than **20 offices** worldwide.

For an embedded-world applicant, Celonis is not a direct embedded-systems fit. It is relevant if the candidate wants to move into SaaS, data platforms, AI, enterprise software, backend engineering, cloud, analytics, customer engineering, or technical consulting.

## Size, revenue, and growth

Celonis is privately held, so audited revenue is not public. The company states **3,000+ employees**, a valuation above **$13B**, and thousands of enterprise deployments. LinkedIn lists the company in the **1,001-5,000 employees** range and shows roughly **3,800 associated employees**.

## Work culture and applicant signal

Employer reviews are good but not uniformly comfortable. Kununu shows around **4.1/5** with **78% recommendation**, and Glassdoor shows around **3.8/5** with **75% recommendation**. Positive signals include strong product, ambitious colleagues, modern culture, brand, and career exposure. Negative signals concentrate around work-life balance, pressure, overtime, senior management, and inconsistent career development.

This can be a good company for ambitious people who want a high-growth SaaS environment and are comfortable with performance pressure. It is less ideal for someone prioritizing predictable hours, a low-stress engineering culture, or deep embedded/low-level technical work.

## Market outlook

The market outlook is strong. Process intelligence, enterprise AI context, automation, and operational efficiency are budget-relevant themes for large companies. Celonis has a recognized category position, but it competes with large enterprise software vendors and must keep proving measurable customer value.
