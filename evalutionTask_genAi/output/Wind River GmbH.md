# Wind River GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - excellent embedded/edge technology brand, but recent culture and stability signals are mixed.

## Applicant Summary

Wind River is a global embedded and intelligent-edge software company known for VxWorks, embedded Linux, cloud-native edge platforms, virtualization, safety-critical systems, and professional services for automotive, aerospace, defense, industrial, medical, and telecom customers. The German entity is part of a globally relevant company with deep technical pedigree. The hiring caveat is current employee sentiment: reviews point to good work-life balance and interesting technology, but also layoffs, management churn, changing culture after Aptiv acquisition, and reduced benefits.

## Location And Departments

- Global headquarters: Walnut Creek, California, USA.
- German entity: Wind River GmbH, exhibiting at embedded world as the German presence.
- Departments/work areas: RTOS/VxWorks, embedded Linux, DevSecOps, cloud-native edge, safety certification, cybersecurity, virtualization, automotive software-defined systems, aerospace/defense, industrial controls, medical devices, telecommunications, professional services, support, and field engineering.
- Remote/mobile work: Glassdoor reviews indicate good work-life balance and some flexible/remote experience, but exact policy depends on country/team.

## Revenue And Growth

Wind River is privately held and does not publish standalone revenue.

- Founded in 1981.
- LinkedIn lists 1,001-5,000 employees.
- The company says its technology powers billions of devices.
- Current growth areas include intelligent edge, edge AI, cloud-native infrastructure, software-defined vehicles, aerospace/defense, and regulated private-cloud/edge deployments.

Interpretation: strategically relevant and technically durable, but public financial data is limited and employee reviews suggest post-acquisition restructuring pressure.

## Market And Goals

The market is strong: safety-critical embedded software, RTOS, edge AI, software-defined vehicles, industrial automation, aerospace/defense, and telecom cloudification are all long-term demand areas. Wind River’s goal is to deliver secure, reliable software for mission-critical intelligent systems and the intelligent edge.

## Employee Satisfaction

- Glassdoor: about 3.4-3.6 score from 652-653 reviews; recommendation ranges around 56-62%; CEO approval around low-to-mid 40s in recent snapshots.
- Positive themes: interesting work, smart people, good work-life balance, strong technical products, good PTO in some regions.
- Negative themes: layoffs/staff changes, Aptiv-related culture change, reduced benefits, bonus uncertainty, salary stagnation, management churn, limited promotions.
- Pay and salary increases: mixed; compensation is not the weakest category, but salary stagnation and bonus concerns appear repeatedly.
- Ambitious people: good for deep embedded/safety-critical software experience; less attractive if the applicant needs fast promotion, high equity upside, or organizational stability.
- Family friendliness: work-life balance signals are relatively positive, but instability risk lowers confidence.

## Decision

Good company for hiring? **Maybe.** Wind River is worth interviewing for high-end embedded, RTOS, Linux, safety, and edge roles. I would directly validate team stability, layoff exposure, bonus history, promotion path, benefits changes, and how Aptiv ownership affects the German team before accepting.
