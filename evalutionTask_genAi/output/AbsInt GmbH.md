# AbsInt GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - strong technical niche, but small-company opacity and no visible active hiring.

## Applicant Summary

AbsInt Angewandte Informatik GmbH is a Saarbruecken-based software tools company for embedded, safety-critical, and security-relevant software verification. It is a specialist vendor rather than a broad embedded service company. For applicants interested in static analysis, compiler technology, WCET analysis, safety standards, and formal methods, the technical fit can be very strong. The main downside is limited public transparency: no standalone revenue data, little public employee-review data, and the official jobs page currently says there are no open positions.

## Location And Departments

- Headquarters: Science Park 1, 66123 Saarbruecken, Germany.
- Departments/work areas: product development, static analysis, compiler/toolchain technology, safety and security certification tooling, consulting, support, training, and research-oriented customer projects.
- Remote/mobile work: no clear public policy found. Assume mostly Saarbruecken-centered unless clarified in interview.

## Revenue And Growth

AbsInt is privately held and does not publish revenue. Public signals point to a stable, long-running niche business rather than high-growth scale-up behavior.

- Founded in 1998 and active for more than 25 years.
- Official material says customers are in more than 40 countries.
- LinkedIn lists 11-50 employees; AbsInt's own staff page says it employs a good 40 people at the Saarbruecken site.

Interpretation: likely stable and specialized, but revenue growth cannot be verified from public sources. The absence of open roles suggests limited hiring velocity.

## Market And Goals

AbsInt works in a durable market: verification and certification of safety-critical embedded software. Demand is supported by automotive ISO 26262, avionics DO-178C, industrial IEC 61508, cybersecurity regulation, and increasing software complexity in embedded systems. This is not a mass-market SaaS growth story, but it is a defensible expert niche with long customer cycles and high technical barriers.
