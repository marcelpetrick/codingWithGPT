# ADASYS GmbH elektronische Komponenten

**Hiring fit:** No - established business, but weak employer-review signals around pay, management, and development.

## Applicant Summary

ADASYS GmbH elektronische Komponenten is a Kornwestheim-based distributor and solution provider for POS hardware, cash register systems, peripherals, tablets, monitors, printers, kiosks, and related electronic components. The company is long-established and backed by Asian electronics-industry ownership through Poindus/Compal, but employer-review data raises meaningful concerns for applicants.

## Location And Departments

- Headquarters: Max-Planck-Strasse 10, 70806 Kornwestheim, Germany.
- Departments/work areas: POS hardware distribution, customer service, project management, sales, logistics/warehouse, technical service, digital signage, embedded PCs/displays, self-checkout/kiosk systems.
- Remote/mobile work: kununu reviews mention flexible hours and some home-office setup, especially in sales, but also criticize outdated internal IT tools.

## Revenue And Growth

ADASYS is private and does not publish reliable standalone revenue.

- Founded as TBN in 1987; ADASYS GmbH formed in 1994.
- Official history says ADASYS had five employees in 1994 and grew to more than 20 employees by the early 2000s.
- LinkedIn lists 11-50 employees.
- Ownership changed in 2017 to Poindus Systems; in 2022 Poindus's parent was taken over by Compal, a major electronics contract manufacturer.

Interpretation: the company appears established and commercially persistent, but there is no public evidence of strong recent growth.

## Market And Goals

ADASYS serves POS, retail, hospitality, logistics, display, and kiosk/self-checkout hardware markets. These markets are stable and can benefit from automation and self-service trends, but they are also competitive and hardware-margin-sensitive. The future-market potential is moderate rather than exceptional.

## Employee Satisfaction

- kununu: 3.0 overall score from 14 reviews; 50% recommendation in the last two years.
- kununu comments include significant criticism of management, internal conflicts, career development, salary fairness, equipment/IT tooling, and communication.
- Salary: kununu salary page shows low satisfaction; only 20% rate salary/benefits as good or very good, and 60% rate pay as bad or very bad.
- Benefits: flexible working hours, parking, company car, mobile phone, and pension are mentioned, but ratings for career/development are weak.
- Candidate experience: kununu applicant feedback is poor, including lack of response.
- Ambitious people: not a strong signal. The role may fit entry-level or hardware/POS sales profiles, but public reviews do not support strong development culture.

## Employee Count

- 2026/current: **11-50** employees on LinkedIn.

- 1994: **5** employees according to company history.

## Decision

Good company for hiring? **No**, unless the applicant has a very specific reason to join a particular team and can validate the manager, salary, tools, and development path directly. The business is established, but the employer data is below the level I would want for a confident recommendation.
