# Tria Technologies GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - strong embedded compute portfolio with Avnet backing, but direct employer-review data is limited.

## Applicant Summary

Tria Technologies GmbH is an embedded computing, systems, and HMI company connected to Avnet Embedded. It designs and manufactures compute modules, single-board computers, full embedded systems, HMIs, BIOS/firmware, operating-system customization, Yocto Linux BSPs, and OEM product integrations. For applicants in embedded hardware, Linux BSPs, BIOS/firmware, HMI systems, production engineering, and OEM-facing embedded platforms, the technical fit is good.

## Location And Departments

- Main German location: Industriestrasse 16, 76297 Stutensee, Germany.
- Additional listed German locations include Poing/Munich, Freiburg, Aachen, Neufahrn, and Wiesbaden, plus international locations in the UK, France, Malta, Sweden, and the US through Avnet Embedded.
- Departments/work areas: compute modules, embedded systems, HMI, custom compute design, system integration, manufacturing, BIOS, Yocto Linux, board support packages, firmware provisioning, OS customization, GUI/app support, quality control, logistics, sales, and customer engineering.
- Remote/mobile work: the careers page lists flexible working options, mobile working, desk sharing, flexitime, trust-based hours, shifts, and part-time depending on department.

## Revenue And Growth

Tria does not publish standalone revenue. It appears to operate within Avnet/Avnet Embedded structures.

- LinkedIn lists the wider Tria/Avnet context as 10,001+ employees, while some localized views show about **279** visible Tria profiles.
- Current company activity includes embedded world 2026, new Intel Core Ultra COM-HPC module launches, Qualcomm OS support, wireless modules, OSM modules, and broad embedded systems/HMI positioning.
- Avnet backing gives global logistics, distribution, and customer access.

Interpretation: strong product/market activity, but standalone financial health and employee counts are not cleanly disclosed.

## Market And Goals

The market is attractive: embedded compute modules, edge AI, industrial automation, robotics, healthcare, agriculture, HMIs, and long-life OEM platforms. Tria’s goal is to help OEMs reach market faster with off-the-shelf or customized embedded compute and full system/HMI solutions.

## Employee Satisfaction

- Tria-specific public employee reviews are sparse or mixed across unrelated “Tria” entities, so I did not treat those as decisive.
- Tria careers page states benefits such as competitive salaries, flexible working, learning/development, subsidized catering, wellbeing program, company pension, employee discounts, events, and healthcare/location-based benefits.
- Positive signals: Avnet backing, modern embedded portfolio, broad technical scope, European design/manufacturing, flexible-working benefits.
- Unknowns: local manager quality, salary progression, career ladder, and culture after brand/group integration.
- Ambitious people: good fit for embedded engineers who want productized hardware/software work at OEM scale; less clear for people seeking pure software roles or startup autonomy.

## Decision

Good company for hiring? **Maybe.** Tria is worth interviewing for embedded compute, HMI, BSP, BIOS, and OEM platform roles. I would validate whether the contract is with Tria/Avnet, the exact team location, salary model, remote policy, career path, and how much of the work is engineering versus production/customer support.
