# dSPACE GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Yes - excellent automotive validation and embedded tooling employer with strong reviews, though salary and career paths should be checked.

## Company snapshot

dSPACE GmbH is headquartered in Paderborn, Germany, and was founded in 1988. It is a major provider of simulation and validation solutions for automotive, software-defined vehicles, autonomous driving, e-mobility, aerospace, agriculture, and industrial automation.

Its portfolio includes HIL/SIL testing, ECU testing, ADAS and radar testing, scenario-based testing, simulation, cybersecurity testing, continuous testing, AI-based testing, data-driven validation, engineering services, consulting, training, and support. For embedded and automotive software applicants, this is a very strong domain fit.

## Size, revenue, and growth

dSPACE is privately held. LinkedIn lists **1,001-5,000 employees**, and the company profile states **more than 2,900 employees worldwide**. Standalone revenue is not publicly disclosed. Growth signals are strong because the company sits directly in software-defined vehicle, validation, ADAS, HIL/SIL, and e-mobility workflows.

## Work culture and applicant signal

The employer signal is strong. Kununu shows around **4.0/5** with **84% recommendation** over the last two years. Glassdoor shows around **4.3/5** with **79% recommendation** and very high CEO approval. Positive signals include work-life balance, colleague cohesion, flexible hours, home office, atmosphere, working conditions, and interesting technical tasks.

Risks are mostly around salary compared with tariff-bound automotive companies, career progression, internal bureaucracy, and team-dependent leadership quality. For applicants, dSPACE is still a strong target if they want deep automotive validation, embedded tooling, test automation, simulation, and product-company engineering rather than consulting-only work.

## Market outlook

The outlook is strong. Software-defined vehicles, autonomous driving, ADAS, electrification, cybersecurity regulation, CI/CT, and validation complexity all increase demand for dSPACE-style tooling and expertise.
