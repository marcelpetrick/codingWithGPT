# Digi International GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Yes - strong public IoT/connectivity company with solid employer reviews, though career progression should be checked.

## Company snapshot

Digi International is a public IoT connectivity company founded in 1985 and headquartered in Hopkins, Minnesota, with German presence listed in Ismaning. The company provides mission-critical IoT and M2M connectivity products and services, including embedded modules, system-on-modules, wireless modules, cellular routers, gateways, network products, Digi Remote Manager, cloud software, and professional IoT services.

For embedded applicants, Digi is relevant across embedded Linux, wireless modules, industrial IoT, edge computing, 5G/LTE routers, device management, cloud-connected hardware, secure remote updates, transportation, medical, agriculture, industrial, and critical infrastructure.

## Size, revenue, and growth

Digi reported fiscal 2025 revenue of **$430M**, up **1%**, with adjusted EBITDA of **$108M** and ARR of **$152M** at year end. First fiscal quarter 2026 revenue reached a record **$122M**, up **18%**, with ARR of **$157M**. LinkedIn lists **501-1,000 employees**.

## Work culture and applicant signal

Employer signals are solid. Glassdoor shows around **3.7/5** with **74% recommendation** and high CEO approval. German Kununu data for Digi International GmbH shows about **3.6/5** with **100% recommendation** over the last two years, but from a small sample. Positives include work-life balance, culture, salary/benefits, flexible hours, and colleague cohesion. The main weaker area is career development.

This looks like a good employer target for applicants who want embedded IoT/connectivity work in a financially stable public company. Applicants should ask about the exact German team, whether the role is sales/application engineering or product engineering, remote policy, and promotion paths.

## Market outlook

The market outlook is good. Secure industrial IoT, edge connectivity, device management, 5G, remote infrastructure, critical communications, and recurring IoT services are all relevant. Digi's rising ARR is a positive business-quality signal.
