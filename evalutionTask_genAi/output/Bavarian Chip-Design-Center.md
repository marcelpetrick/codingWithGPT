# Bavarian Chip-Design-Center

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - excellent chip-design mission, but hiring is likely through Fraunhofer/university partners rather than BCDC itself.

## Applicant Summary

The Bavarian Chip-Design-Center (BCDC) is a publicly funded competence center for chip design in Bavaria, operated by Fraunhofer AISEC, Fraunhofer EMFT, and Fraunhofer IIS together with Bavarian universities and colleges. It is not a conventional private employer. For applicants interested in IC design, RISC-V, chiplets, sensor/actuator systems, AI processors, supply-chain access, and semiconductor training, the technical mission is highly relevant.

## Location And Departments

- Address/contact: Am Wolfsmantel 33, 91058 Erlangen, Germany; embedded world profile also lists Erlangen.
- Operators: Fraunhofer AISEC, Fraunhofer EMFT, Fraunhofer IIS.
- Academic partners include TUM, FAU, HM, TH Nuremberg, and OTH Regensburg.
- Program areas: IC Design Talents, IC Supply Chain, IC Design Ecosystem.
- Technical areas: sensor and actuator systems, AI, RISC-V, chiplet-based systems, secure SoC, RF sensor systems, ultra-low-power receivers, ASIC/system design, prototype and small-series access, IC testing.
- Remote/mobile work: depends on the actual hiring institution; likely research-project and lab/site dependent.

## Revenue And Growth

BCDC is publicly funded and does not have conventional revenue.

- Launched in 2022 with an initial grant of around EUR 1M.
- In January 2024, Bavaria approved an additional EUR 50M grant.
- The project is funded by the Bavarian State Ministry of Economic Affairs, Regional Development and Energy, with federal support in the context of the Microelectronics Academy/FMD work.

Interpretation: not a commercial growth company, but strongly funded and strategically important for Bavaria/Germany's semiconductor capability.

## Market And Goals

The field is very strong. Europe is investing heavily in semiconductor sovereignty, supply-chain resilience, IC design capability, RISC-V, AI accelerators, and chiplet systems. BCDC's goal is to strengthen Bavarian chip-design capabilities and give startups and SMEs easier access to chip design and supply chains.
