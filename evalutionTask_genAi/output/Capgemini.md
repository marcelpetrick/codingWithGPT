# Capgemini

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - broad engineering career platform and strong finances, but consulting bench risk and current restructuring make role/team selection critical.

## Company snapshot

Capgemini is a global consulting, technology, and engineering services group headquartered in Paris. For embedded applicants, the relevant unit is usually Capgemini Engineering, which works across automotive, aerospace, industrial manufacturing, software-defined products, ADAS, AUTOSAR, C/C++, RTOS, ASPICE, test automation, and systems engineering.

Capgemini is not a pure embedded company. It is a large services employer where the quality of the job depends strongly on business unit, client project, location, manager, and whether the role is delivery, consulting, architecture, or staff augmentation.

## Size, revenue, and growth

Capgemini reported **EUR 22.465B** revenue in 2025, up **1.7%** reported and **3.4%** at constant exchange rates. Bookings were **EUR 24.4B** with a **1.08** book-to-bill. Headcount at the end of 2025 was **423,400**, significantly increased by the WNS acquisition.

## Work culture and applicant signal

The employer signal is mixed. Capgemini offers global mobility, many domains, training, recognizable brand value, and access to large clients. Embedded roles can be genuinely attractive when they sit in Capgemini Engineering teams with stable automotive, aerospace, or industrial accounts.

The risk is the consulting model. Applicants should check utilization pressure, bench policy, travel expectations, remote policy, project continuity, salary band, and whether the role is actually embedded engineering or generic IT delivery. Recent European restructuring news and staffing adjustments increase the need to verify team stability before accepting.

This is a good target for applicants who want a broad consulting career and can manage project changes. It is less attractive for someone seeking a focused product-company environment with long-term ownership of one codebase.

## Market outlook

The overall outlook is moderate to good. Capgemini benefits from AI, cloud, data, engineering services, digital manufacturing, and software-defined product programs, but client spending is selective and cost-sensitive. Embedded/software-defined vehicle work remains relevant, though consulting demand can be cyclical.
