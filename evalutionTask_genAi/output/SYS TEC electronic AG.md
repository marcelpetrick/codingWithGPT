# SYS TEC electronic AG

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - relevant embedded hardware/software work, but employer reviews show meaningful culture and career-development risk.

## Company snapshot

SYS TEC electronic AG is a German electronics system house in Heinsdorfergrund, Saxony. It develops and manufactures customer-specific electronic systems, covering consulting, hardware development, embedded software, prototyping, testing, certification support, and series production. Its markets include industrial automation, communication, railway, automotive, off-road machinery, medical, safety applications, CANopen, smart PLCs, edge control, and smart industry.

For an embedded applicant, the technical scope is relevant and practical: hardware-near software, industrial communication, embedded hardware, safety-oriented projects, and manufacturing transfer from prototype to series.

## Work culture and applicant signal

The employer signal is mixed. SYS TEC presents itself as a continuously growing, established medium-sized company with modern working conditions, performance-related remuneration, onboarding support, and development opportunities. Kununu, however, shows only around **3.0/5** with a low recommendation rate. Positive themes include interesting tasks, working conditions, and colleague cohesion. Negative themes include career development, internal communication, management, and compensation recognition.

This can still be a reasonable choice for applicants who want hands-on embedded systems in a smaller Saxony-based manufacturer, especially if they value varied technical work over corporate career ladders. It is less attractive if salary progression, structured leadership, remote work, or fast promotion are priorities.

## Market outlook

SYS TEC operates in durable industrial niches: automation, machine communication, safety electronics, embedded control, and electronics manufacturing services. The market is stable, but competition is high and margins can be tighter than in pure software, semiconductor IP, or premium automotive toolchains.
