# Dream Chip Technologies GmbH

**Hiring fit:** Maybe - very strong semiconductor and embedded technology fit, but culture data is mixed and Tessolve integration should be checked.

## Company snapshot

Dream Chip Technologies GmbH is a Garbsen-based semiconductor and embedded engineering company. It describes itself as one of Germany's largest independent turnkey engineering service providers for ASICs, SoCs, FPGAs, embedded software, and complex systems. Its focus areas include automotive HPC, functional-safety image processing, HDR camera products, custom vision SoCs, FPGA/ASIC development, embedded software, tape-out, and post-silicon bring-up.

Since February 2025, Dream Chip has been part of the Tessolve group, which broadens its turnkey semiconductor service capability.

## Size, revenue, and growth

LinkedIn lists Dream Chip in the **51-200 employees** range and shows roughly **120+ associated employees**. Standalone revenue is not publicly disclosed. The Tessolve acquisition and recent investment in Siemens Veloce Strato CS emulation infrastructure are positive growth and capability signals.

## Work culture and applicant signal

The technology signal is stronger than the employer-review signal. Kununu has a very small sample around **3.4/5** and **67% recommendation**, with mixed feedback: good climate and high-tech work on the positive side, but criticism around communication, structure, use of employee strengths, and development support. Glassdoor has very little data and is too small to rely on.

This is potentially attractive for ambitious semiconductor, embedded software, FPGA, SoC, automotive vision, and verification engineers. The main diligence points are team structure after Tessolve integration, whether the role is internal R&D or external customer delivery, salary, training budget, project assignment, and management communication.

## Market outlook

The market outlook is strong. Europe wants more semiconductor design capability, automotive HPC is expanding, vision systems remain important, and ASIC/SoC verification capacity is valuable. Dream Chip sits in a technically attractive niche, especially with Tessolve backing.
