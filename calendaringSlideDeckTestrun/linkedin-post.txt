Most engineering teams use calendars backwards.

They optimize for availability.
Not output.

That is how you get:

1. Constant interruptions.
2. Fragmented build time.
3. Stakeholders chasing people directly.
4. Meetings creeping back into every open slot.
5. Focus time that exists in theory, but not in practice.

The fix is not "no meetings".
The fix is a clearer operating system:

- Focus windows that are treated as real commitments.
- Collaboration windows where teams can talk.
- Stakeholder windows for reviews and decisions.
- Explicit rules for what "busy", "focus", and "urgent" mean.

Try one small change tomorrow:

Block 09:00-11:00 as Focus.
Decline meetings in that window.
Use the script:
"Can we move this? I keep mornings for build work."

Calendars are not admin.
They are boundaries made visible.
