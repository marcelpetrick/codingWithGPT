Calendaring is not admin work. It is how teams make time visible.

When a calendar is kept clean, people no longer have to guess when you are available, when you need focus, or what is actually important this week.

The five habits that make it work:

1. Show real availability.
2. Protect focus time like a meeting.
3. Make collaboration easier across teams and time zones.
4. Turn priorities into time blocks.
5. Reduce back-and-forth and mental load.

The tool matters less than the behavior: one calendar, clear event names, honest availability, and a weekly review.

Calendaring done right is not about being busy.
It is about making work easier to coordinate.
